Project and Challenge Guidelines

It is great that you want to propose a project or challenge for the National Day of Civic Hacking. Below are a set of general guidelines and key components for potential projects - things that will make you successful at NDoCH in San Francisco.

Steps to Pitch Your Project at NDoCH

  • Read this entire page to learn more about what to expect and the timeline of the event.
  • Design a project for NDoCH using the resources here.
  • Fill out a project pitch form.
  • Receive confirmation that your project has been accepted.
  • Fill in all details for your project on our hackathon platform.
  • If participants contact you about interest in your project, be available to respond before NDoCH.
  • Come ready to pitch your project on Friday Night and recruit your team!

Project Focus

The theme of this event is how to to hack our civic space, the city of San Francisco. Project should be focused on improving our city in some way. The topics areas for projects might include open data, disaster relief, mapping, safety and justice, economic development, and whatever else.

Project Lead

Each project requires a project lead to see the project from start to finish, attending the 3-day event. It is okay for two people to collaboratively lead the team.

Goals and Structure

The project proposals for the weekend should:

  • Have tangible goals that can be met by a team of 4-10 people over 2 full days of work
  • Use varied skills and expertise levels
  • Clearly articulate challenges and tangible goals
  • Be scalable and flexible to fit the team you can recruit to your project
  • Be open and collaborative (using tools like GitHub, Hackpad, Trello, etc to share your work)

Propose a project

Have a project that meets the guidelines? Great! Submit your project here and don't forget to register for the event as well. If you have any other questions, contact hello@codeforsanfransico.com

Timeline

  • Friday, June 5th

    Pitch Your Project: During the Opening Party, you will be given a station to share your project idea, like a trade show. Participants will circle around and chat with you throughout the evening to learn more about the projects. As people circulate, they will also sign up for specific projects, so you will have a concrete idea as to who will be on your project team at the end of Friday Night.

  • Saturday, June 6th

    Work Day: This whole day is devoted to project work. There will be experts on hand to consult about specific topics. Civic User Testing will be conducted, and that data can be used as a resource. There will also be a number of fun activities to take a break from work and have fun with other participants.

  • Sunday, June 7th

    Finishing Work: The morning and early afternoon will be used for project to continue and come to a conclusion. Each project should also prepare a short presentation.

    Final Presentations: With a set of judges, each project shares their work to the group. Top projects will win a prize.

    Closing Reception: There will be a closing reception for all attendees and guests. Drinks and snacks will be provided.
