Project and Challenge Guidelines

It is great that you want to propose a project or challenge for the National Day of Civic Hacking. This covers the eligibility of projects, requirements for the project structure, steps to pitch a project, and general goals that each project should accomplish over the weekend. This guidelines will make a succesful project.

Eligibility

Focus: Project should be focused on improving our city in some way. The topics areas for projects might include open data, disaster relief, mapping, safety and justice, economic development, and whatever else.

Starting Status: Projects can be at different phases (ideation, testing a specific component, a few steps before launch, etc.) With that, structure the weekendâ€™s project proposal so that new individuals can contribute to a specific goal or milestone.

Project Lead: Each project requires a project lead to see the project from start to finish, attending the 3-day event. It is okay for two people to collaboratively lead the team (and split the schedule) provided they cohesively tie out with one another.

Team Size: You can enter as an individual with a project to recruit a team, or enter as a team with a project. With that, you must be open to having new individuals throughout the weekend.

Skills of Attendees: Developers, project managers, community organizers, designers, researchers, marketers, government staff, urbanites, and generally active and knowledgeable citizens.

Requirements

A project must
  • Be open source and collaborative (using tools like GitHub, Hackpad, Trello, and others to share your work)
  • Have a clear problem defined
  • Have articulated and tangible goals that can be met by a team of 4-10 people over 2 full days of work
  • Use varied skills and expertise levels
  • Be scalable and flexible to fit the team you can recruit to your project

Steps to Pitch Your Project at NDoCH

  1. Read this entire page to learn more about what to expect and the timeline of the event, including the commitment that you will be present to lead your project at the hackathon for the entire weekend
  2. Design a project for NDoCH using the resources here.
  3. Fill out a project pitch form.
  4. Receive confirmation that your project has been accepted.
  5. Detail your project out on Challenge Post for attendees to see it before the event.
  6. Respond to inquiries about your project leading up to NDoCH, whether by interested attendees or by press inquiries.
  7. Pitch on Friday Night (trade show style) to attendees and recruit your team.
  8. Work Saturday and Sunday (with final presentations Sunday Night).
  9. Present the team's final work on Sunday afternoon to a panel of judges.

Judging Critera

Minimum project goals:
  • Identify users
  • Show supporting data
  • Complete a competitive assessment
  • Have either an MVP AND/OR concrete conceptual plan
  • List your next steps
  • Show potential civic and social partners

Propose a project

Have a project that meets the guidelines? Great! Submit your project here and don't forget to register for the event as well. If you have any other questions, contact hello@codeforsanfransico.com

Timeline

  • Friday, June 5th

    Pitch Your Project: During the Opening Party, you will be given a station to share your project idea, like a trade show. Participants will circle around and chat with you throughout the evening to learn more about the projects. As people circulate, they will also sign up for specific projects, so you will have a concrete idea as to who will be on your project team at the end of Friday Night.

  • Saturday, June 6th

    Work Day: This whole day is devoted to project work. There will be experts on hand to consult about specific topics. Civic User Testing will be conducted, and that data can be used as a resource. There will also be a number of fun activities to take a break from work and have fun with other participants.

  • Sunday, June 7th

    Finishing Work: The morning and early afternoon will be used for project to continue and come to a conclusion. Each project should also prepare a short presentation.

    Final Presentations: With a set of judges, each project shares their work to the group. Top projects will win a prize.

    Closing Reception: There will be a closing reception for all attendees and guests. Drinks and snacks will be provided.
